Class 7 English — Reading and Writing Skills (SAMPLE, synthetic outline)

This is a synthetic sample outline for the Teacher Copilot demo, not taken from any
copyrighted textbook.

Reading comprehension
Students read short prose passages and poems and answer questions that test literal
understanding, inference, and vocabulary in context. Encourage learners to underline
key sentences and to guess word meanings from surrounding text before reaching for a
dictionary.

Paragraph writing
A good paragraph has one main idea stated in a topic sentence, followed by supporting
sentences with details and examples, and a closing sentence. Students practise writing
descriptive paragraphs about familiar topics such as a festival, a market, or a rainy
day, focusing on clear structure and correct punctuation.

Grammar in use
Tenses (simple present, past, and future), subject-verb agreement, and common
prepositions of place and time. Grammar is taught in the context of the students' own
writing rather than as isolated drills.

Speaking and listening
Short show-and-tell activities and pair discussions build confidence in spoken English.
Listening tasks use simple audio or teacher read-alouds followed by comprehension
questions.
